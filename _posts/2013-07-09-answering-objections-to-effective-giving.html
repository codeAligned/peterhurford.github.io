All the aid is squandered by corrupt bureaucrats. But giving still accomplishes litte, because the organizations squander it on overhead. imagine an org with no overhead - no electricity, volunteer flashlights, etc. If I really want to help someone, I'm better off giving the money directly to a homeless person. But look how much we spend on foreign aid! Surely if it were effective, problems would have been solved by now. 1.) Aid isn't that much + wrong guesses about aid 2.) Problems have been solved I don't know much about the problems in Africa. I don't want to be a "white in shining armor". But I do know about the problems in my home town, so I'm going to help here. Charity starts at home, right? But morality isn't about giving to the third world. It's about being a good person and upholding virtue. I like my own local community better. I want to give back. Why shouldn't I just donate locally? You can't save a life. If I give a person an anti-malaria bednet, they'll just die of another disease in a few months, right? But really, you can't save a life. Everyone will die eventually. So what's the point? Okay, so maybe I'd be happy to "extend lives" or "save lives" or whatever you want to call it. But all that will do is cause overpopulation, and that will lead to a whole host of other problems. Shouldn't we be attacking "root causes" and not mere symptoms? Giving to the third world is fine, but if we're taking this long-term view, aren't there even better opportunities? Aid will just breed dependency. I have a right to keep my own money. I worked hard for it. Why should I give it to other people who haven't worked hard for it? The problem is too large. I can't dent it. Ok, so maybe I'm making some sort of dent. But how can I know? The problem is so large, I can't see my impact. How do we know we're doing good? We don't need aid. We need political reform. It's the system that's bringing us down. In fact, philanthropy makes it harder to bring about this reform. Giving personally is a needlessly conservative idea that ignores the benefits of public coordination and reform. I don't earn enough money to afford to give to charity.
