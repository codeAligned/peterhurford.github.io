--- layout: post title: But Really, Can Money Buy You Happiness? categories: - All tags: [] status: draft type: post published: false meta: _edit_last: '1' _s2mail: 'yes' _jd_tweet_this: 'yes' --- It's something of folk wisdom that "money can't buy happiness". However, like most folk wisdom, it has been shattered through scientific analysis. Or has it? The problem is that there has been a lot of attention to the connection between how much money one has and how happy they are, and different investigation comes to slightly different conclusions. But why might this be the case? I decided I was confused enough about the relationship between money and happiness that I wanted to look at all these studies and try and get to the bottom of things.

 

What Correlates with Happiness?

One thing that is clear is if money has some correlation with happiness, it's one correlate among many. Interestingly and perhaps frustratingly, ones genes account for 38%[A] to to 80%[B] of the variation between happiness among people, with most studies settling on 50%[C].
